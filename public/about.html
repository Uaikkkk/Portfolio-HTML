About Me

I was born in January 1, 1996 in a small village called Rantau Prapat, Sumatra Utara, Indonesia.

I went to the closest city from my village, so that I could get a better education and managed to graduate from my high school Sutomo-1 Medan in 2013. After graduating, I decided to go to University College Dublin in Singapore to continue for my college degree. I took Finance and Accounting, but the main reason I want to be in Singapore is to improve my English speaking skills while also exposing myself to a more diverse and international environment. After, I have graduated from my university in Singapore, I have become fluent in speaking English and wanted to learn one more new language. So, I finally decided to come to Japan and started learning basic Japanese for one year in a Japanese language school called The Asian Student Culture Association. Then, I continued going to Tokyo University of Agriculture to learn more about agribusiness.

Now, I am working with Seven Global Remit in promoting a remittance transfer app called Sendy. My main responsibilities are translation/interpretation (English, Japanese and Bahasa Indonesia), marketing, sales, customer service and the app development. While working on this project, my interest towards IT industry keeps on growing. Thus, I am planning to transition into the IT world by taking my first step in learning the basics through online classes.
